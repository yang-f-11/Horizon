## [Xiaomi OneVL: Open-Source Latent Reasoning Framework for Autonomous Driving](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/7po3r6YtmuXm8Xny1bw61Q) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Xiaomi has released and fully open-sourced OneVL, a stepwise latent space reasoning framework for autonomous driving that unifies Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models and world models. It achieves state-of-the-art results on four benchmarks, including a PDM-score of 88.84 on NAVSIM, surpassing explicit chain-of-thought (CoT) for the first time in latent space reasoning. OneVL is the first latent CoT method to outperform explicit CoT in autonomous driving, offering up to 95% latency reduction compared to autoregressive VLA models. This breakthrough could enable faster and more efficient real-time decision-making in autonomous vehicles, advancing the field of end-to-end driving. OneVL uses visual latent tokens to encode physical causal structures and language latent tokens for driving intentions, with dual auxiliary decoders removed during inference for single-step parallel generation. When combined with an MLP regression head, latency is reduced to 0.24 seconds, just 5.4% of autoregressive VLA inference.

telegram · zaihuapd · May 13, 10:33

**Background**: Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models integrate perception, reasoning, and control for autonomous driving but typically rely on autoregressive chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning, which is slow and computationally expensive. Latent space reasoning compresses reasoning steps into a compact representation, enabling faster inference without explicit intermediate outputs. World models predict future states, adding an understanding of physics and causality.

## [Guide to Free Locality Domains Under .us TLD (2025)](https://fredchan.org/blog/locality-domains-guide/) ⭐️ 7.0/10

A detailed guide published in May 2025 explains how to obtain free locality domains under the .us TLD, using Amazon Lightsail for nameservers and contacting delegated locality managers via the Interim .US Domain Template. Locality domains offer free, unique naming options tied to specific cities and states, but the registration process can be opaque and bureaucratic, making this guide valuable for developers and local organizations. There are over 7,000 locality domains, each managed by a delegated organization; registration requires satisfying the U.S. Nexus requirement, and .us domains forbid WHOIS privacy services, which raises privacy concerns.

hackernews · speckx · May 13, 14:45 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48122635)

**Background**: The .us TLD is the country-code top-level domain for the United States, managed by Neustar (now part of GoDaddy). Locality domains are second-level subdomains such as city.state.us, delegated to local registrars (often ISPs, universities, or local governments). To register one, you typically need to find the delegated manager for your locality and submit a registration template.

The experiment uses a sandboxed iframe with a strict CSP (default-src 'none') and overrides fetch() to catch connections blocked by CSP's connect-src directive, passing them to the parent window for user approval.

**Background**: Content Security Policy (CSP) is a security standard that helps prevent cross-site scripting (XSS) and other code injection attacks by specifying which sources of content are allowed. Traditionally, CSP errors are reported via reports or blocked silently; this experiment demonstrates a novel way to intercept those errors.
